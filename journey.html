- Mary Oliver -

I received my bachelor's degree in Production Engineering in 2012.

By that time, I had already accumulated 4 years of full-time effective work experience in the area, unlike many of my peers.

With my diploma in hands, I felt the need to venture into different seas. I applied to one of the most challanging recruitment processes in Brazil to become a Business Consultant.

After two months confined in a hotel with my competitors, it was announced that I was in the group of people selected to take the positions availalbe.

My mission was to enable innovation in small and medium-sized companies. I made use of strategies that included introduction of new technologies, rethinking business models and changing marketing strategies.

My next adventrue took place in the second largest Brazilian broadcasting company, RBS.

My commitment to meeting the company's targets rendered me a 100% sponsorship to undertake a 2-year postgraduation course.

However, the wind changed its direction, and I abandoned the course in response to the most awaited call to move to Australia.

EGR had me in the midst of their business reestructure.

As a Business Process Analyst, I was involved in several ICT projects that enabled company's digital transformation. There couldn't have been a more challenging place to become an expert in eliciting requirements and designing solutions.

However, the development team wasn't keeping up with the volume of projects and I really wanted to help...

I decided I wanted to study programing to eventually help develop my own BA projects.

In the end of 2021 I had to leave EGR due to family obligations and I took the opportunity to undertake a postgraduation study in IT.

I am currently amazed with the amount of knowledge this opportunity is offering me. It is striking to see the advantages of being both an analyst and a programmer.
